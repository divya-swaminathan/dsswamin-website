About

I’m drawn to problems where data, money, and global scale meet: streaming and batch pipelines, workflow orchestration across long-running jobs, configuration that must stay consistent across partitions and regions, and on-call tooling that makes failures diagnosable. On Bedrock Commerce, that shows up as metering, allocation, and settlement workflows that have to stay accurate through model launches, region expansion, and hard operational deadlines.

I’ve been at Amazon for nine years and counting. I spend a lot of time on architecture reviews, design docs, and mentoring — turning ambiguous requirements into systems with clear failure modes and measurable behavior. Earlier, I built and operated Amazon Kinesis Video Streams control and data-plane features, including public AWS APIs and fleet-wide infrastructure change under strict availability targets.

Outside of work I’m a dog person (golden retriever, Splash), a parent to a three-year-old, and I reset with racquetball, Seattle-area hiking, summer walks, books, and travel when I can.
